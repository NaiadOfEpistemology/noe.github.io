Firewalls and Web Security

Packet Filters

Packet filtering is a firewall technique that controls network traffic based on predefined rules. It examines packet headers (IP addresses, ports, protocols) and allows or blocks traffic accordingly.

Advantages:

  • Fast and efficient
  • Operates at the network layer

Disadvantages:

  • Limited protection against advanced threats
  • No deep packet inspection

Application-Level Gateways

An Application-Level Gateway (ALG), also known as a proxy firewall, filters traffic at the application layer. It inspects and manages data before forwarding it, adding an extra security layer.

Key Features:

  • Prevents direct client-server connections
  • Provides detailed control over data flow
  • Slower compared to packet filters

Encrypted Tunnels

Encrypted tunnels protect data transmission by encapsulating network traffic within secure, encrypted channels. VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) and SSH tunnels are common examples.

Benefits:

  • Prevents data interception
  • Ensures privacy over untrusted networks

Cookies

Cookies store small pieces of user data on a browser, enabling session management and personalization. However, they also pose security risks such as cross-site scripting (XSS) and session hijacking.

Security Measures:

  • Use HTTP-only and secure cookies
  • Implement same-site restrictions
  • Regularly clear and manage cookies

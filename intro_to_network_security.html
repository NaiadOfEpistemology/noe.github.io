Introduction to Network Security

Overview

Network security involves measures taken to protect the integrity, confidentiality, and availability of data and resources while being transmitted over or stored on networking systems. These measures encompass hardware, software, policies, and procedures to defend against threats, attacks, and unauthorized access.

Importance

With the increasing reliance on networks for communication, commerce, and information storage, the need for robust network security has never been greater. Effective network security ensures data privacy, protects against cyber attacks, and helps maintain the trust of users and stakeholders.

Goals

The primary goals of network security are:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring that information is accessible only to those authorized to have access.
  • Integrity: Safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods.
  • Availability: Ensuring that authorized users have access to information and associated assets when required.
